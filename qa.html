what's the story behind the licence plate on your wall?

in 2016 I went abroad for a year in Durham, UK.

during my time there I bought a lot of shit including a digital piano, a drum kit and a bike.

when my year abroad came to an end, I was faced with the question of how to get everything home.

I crunched some numbers and figured that it would be cheaper to buy a run-down van and drive back to Germany than to ship everything and take the train or plane.

so I borrowed 500 quid from my mum (thank you so much for your financial support, I wouldn't know what I'd do without!) and took the train to Guiseley (near Leeds) and bought a used Citroen Berlingo.

after driving around a bit in a residential area to get used to sitting on the right-hand side of a vehicle, I drove the 80 miles home.

the car didn't have a stereo, so I blasted Saosin's In Search of Solid Ground on my mini JBL speaker.

bought a car radio on Gumtree a couple of days later.

the seller was a bit shady, apparaently he had just bought the car a couple of days earlier and didn't have the new V5C yet and gave me an already used vehicle ownership transfer slip from the old owner's V5C. also, he asked me to remove the signage of his electrical company that was still on the van.

figured this out after doing a bit of research and finding the ad from the old owner on Gumtree with whom I've had a phone call and who was also shocked by the backhanded behaviour of the seller.

long story short, drove the van back to Germany and a couple of days later it died on the Autobahn.

the cooling fluid was leaking and I forgot to fill it up so I wrecked the engine.

phoned up a couple of scrapyards and found one that would still give me 50 euros for the van so I asked my best pal to tow me to the scrapyard.

on the way there, when making a left turn, my pal cut someone off who drove onto the towing rope so I was pulled right into his driver side.

my mum towed me to the scrapyard a couple of days later, kept the car radio and the licence plates.

maths

one of your favourite proofs?

it's a simple one from graph theory.

I'm gonna word it in more understandable terms than graph theory lingo.

statement: at any party, there must be at least two people who have greeted the same number of people.

proof by contradiction:

  • let's assume the opposite: that everyone at the party has greeted a different number of people.
  • there are N people at the party.
  • the number of people greeted can lie in the range from 0 to N‚Äì1.
  • that's N choices for N people.
  • by the pigeonhole principle, that means for any of those numbers, there would be one person who has greeted that number of people.
  • but that would mean there's one person who has greeted nobody (0) and one person who has greeted everyone else (N‚Äì1) which is a logical contradiction.
  • assuming falseness of the statement yields a contradiction, hence the statement must be true.
